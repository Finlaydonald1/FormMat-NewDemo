The problem

Sedentary study habits are silently harming student health.

Long hours at desks, exam stress and remote learning mean students are sitting more than ever. Most tools track steps—not the hours of static, slouched posture that drive back pain, fatigue and long‑term health risks.

Why sitting still is more dangerous than it looks

Sedentary time is now considered an independent risk factor—separate from how much you exercise. You can hit the gym and still be at risk if you spend the rest of the day glued to a chair.

  • Reduced blood flow and muscle activity increase the risk of cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes.
  • Static, slouched posture compresses the spine, leading to back and neck pain, headaches and poor focus.
  • Without feedback, most students underestimate how long they’ve been sitting—and how often they should move.
FormMat is built to support UN SDG 3: Good Health & Wellbeing by turning everyday study spaces into active, responsive environments. Instead of nagging you to “work out more”, it nudges you to sit better, move more often and listen to your body—right where you already are.
The solution

A smart mat that behaves like a home personal trainer.

FormMat combines a pressure‑sensing mat with your device camera to understand how you sit, stand and move—then coaches you with simple, science‑backed micro‑breaks throughout the day.

How it works

Camera + pressure mat = your virtual home trainer.

FormMat turns everyday devices into a coaching system that understands your posture and movement—without needing a gym, wearables or complicated setups.

1
Place the mat & position your camera

Lay the pressure mat under your feet or chair and point your laptop or phone camera towards your upper body. FormMat calibrates in under 30 seconds to learn your neutral posture and stance.

2
FormMat tracks posture and pressure in real time

As you study, the camera analyses your spine alignment while the mat senses how your weight shifts. When it detects slouching, asymmetry or long periods of stillness, it quietly flags it in your dashboard.

3
Your “personal trainer” nudges you to move

When it’s time, FormMat suggests short, guided micro‑moves—like standing resets, ankle‑pumps or shoulder rolls—tailored to what your camera and mat are seeing. You get the benefits of a trainer, right on your floor.
